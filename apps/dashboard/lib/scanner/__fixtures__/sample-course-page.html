Bachelor of Engineering in Civil Engineering

The Bachelor of Engineering in Civil Engineering is a comprehensive 4-year programme that equips students with the knowledge and skills to design, construct, and maintain infrastructure essential for modern society. Graduates are prepared for careers in construction, transportation, water resources, and urban development.

Admission Requirements

Minimum Entry Requirements

  • APS: 34 points minimum
  • Mathematics: Level 6 (70%+)
  • Physical Science: Level 5 (60%+)
  • English: Level 4 (50%+)

Additional Requirements

  • National Senior Certificate with Bachelor's pass
  • No subjects below Level 4
  • Life Orientation not counted in APS

Curriculum Overview

Year 1

  • Engineering Mathematics I
  • Physics for Engineers
  • Introduction to Civil Engineering
  • Engineering Graphics
  • Chemistry for Engineers

Year 2

  • Engineering Mathematics II
  • Mechanics of Materials
  • Surveying
  • Fluid Mechanics
  • Structural Analysis I

Year 3

  • Structural Analysis II
  • Geotechnical Engineering
  • Transportation Engineering
  • Water Resources Engineering
  • Construction Management

Year 4

  • Structural Design
  • Environmental Engineering
  • Engineering Economics
  • Final Year Project
  • Professional Practice

Career Opportunities

  • Structural Engineer
  • Construction Manager
  • Transportation Engineer
  • Water Resources Engineer
  • Urban Planner
  • Environmental Consultant
